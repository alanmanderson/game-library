# Pinochle Rules

## Overview

Pinochle is a trick-taking card game played with a 48-card double deck. The most common format is **4-player partnership** (two teams of two), though 2-player and 3-player variants exist. Each hand consists of bidding, melding, and trick-taking. The first team to reach **150 points** wins.

---

## The Deck

A Pinochle deck contains **two copies** of each of the following cards in all four suits (Spades, Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs):

**Ace, Ten, King, Queen, Jack, Nine**

Total: 48 cards (12 unique cards × 4 suits = 24 × 2 copies = 48)

---

## Card Rankings

From highest to lowest within any suit:

**Ace > Ten > King > Queen > Jack > Nine**

Trump cards beat all non-trump cards of any rank. Within trump, the same ranking applies.

---

## Setup

### Players and Teams
- 4 players seated alternately (partners sit across from each other).
- North/South vs. East/West.

### Dealing
- Dealer is chosen randomly; deal rotates clockwise each hand.
- Deal all 48 cards, **4 cards at a time**, clockwise until each player holds **12 cards**.

---

## Bidding

Starting with the player to the dealer's left, each player either bids or passes. Bids represent the minimum number of points the bidder's team commits to scoring (meld + tricks combined) that hand.

- **Minimum bid:** 20
- Bids increase by increments of 1 (or more).
- A player who passes may not re-enter the auction.
- The **highest bidder** wins the bid and names trump.
- If all three players pass, the dealer is forced to bid 20.

---

## Naming Trump

The winning bidder declares one of the four suits as **trump**. All cards of that suit outrank all non-trump cards for the duration of the hand.

### Passing (Partnership)
After trump is named, the winning bidder's partner passes **4 cards** face-down to the bidder. The bidder reviews them, then passes **4 different cards** back. This allows the partnership to consolidate strong melds and game-winning cards before melding.

---

## Melding

All players lay down cards from their hand face-up on the table to score meld points. Cards may be used in multiple melds (e.g., a King can be part of a Marriage and Kings Around simultaneously), **except** that a card used in a Run may not also be used for a trump Marriage.

After meld is scored and recorded, all meld cards are returned to each player's hand.

### Meld Scoring Table

| Meld | Description | Points |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Trump Nine (Dix)** | Nine of trump | 1 |
| **Marriage** | K + Q of the same non-trump suit | 2 |
| **Royal Marriage** | K + Q of trump | 4 |
| **Pinochle** | J♦ + Q♠ | 4 |
| **Jacks Around** | One J of each suit | 4 |
| **Queens Around** | One Q of each suit | 6 |
| **Kings Around** | One K of each suit | 8 |
| **Aces Around** | One A of each suit | 10 |
| **Run (Flush)** | A-10-K-Q-J of trump | 15 |
| **Double Pinochle** | J♦J♦ + Q♠Q♠ | 30 |
| **Double Jacks Around** | Two J of each suit | 40 |
| **Double Queens Around** | Two Q of each suit | 60 |
| **Double Kings Around** | Two K of each suit | 80 |
| **Double Aces Around** | Two A of each suit | 100 |
| **Double Run** | Two complete A-10-K-Q-J of trump | 150 |

> **Note on Doubles:** Double melds require both copies of each card. A Double Run subsumes the Royal Marriage and both trump Nines (those are not counted separately). Similarly, Double Aces Around subsumes Aces Around — score only the highest applicable combination.

---

## Trick-Taking

### Rules of Play
1. The winning bidder leads the first trick.
2. Play proceeds clockwise.
3. **Players must follow suit if able.**
4. If a player cannot follow suit, they **must play a trump card** if they have one.
5. If a player has neither the led suit nor trump, they may play any card.
6. A trick is won by the highest trump played, or — if no trump — by the highest card of the led suit.
7. If two identical cards are played to the same trick, the one played **first** wins.
8. The winner of each trick leads the next.

### Collecting Tricks
Winning tricks are collected face-down by one partner and kept in a single pile per team. Only the card values matter — the number of tricks does not.

---

## Scoring

### Trick Points

| Card | Points |
|------|--------|
| Ace | 1 |
| Ten | 1 |
| King | 1 |
| Queen | 0 |
| Jack | 0 |
| Nine | 0 |
| **Last trick** | **1** |

With a double deck, there are **25 total trick points** available per hand (8 Aces + 8 Tens + 8 Kings + 1 last trick).

### Making vs. Going Set

At the end of a hand:

- **Bidding team:** Add meld points and trick points together.
  - If the total **≥ bid**, the bidding team scores all their meld + trick points.
  - If the total **< bid**, the bidding team **goes set**: they score **zero** for the hand and subtract their bid from their cumulative score (a team's score may go negative).
- **Non-bidding team:** Always scores their meld + trick points, regardless of outcome.

> A team that takes **zero tricks** scores **zero trick points and zero meld points** for the hand.

---

## Winning

The first team to reach **150 cumulative points** at the end of a hand wins the game.

- If both teams reach 150 in the same hand, the **bidding team wins**.
- A team may only win on a hand in which they took at least one trick.
- If a team goes set and would have otherwise crossed 150, they do not win — their score is reduced by the bid instead.

---

## Variant: 2-Player Pinochle

The same rules apply with the following changes:
- Deal 12 cards to each player; place the remaining 24 face-down as a **draw pile**, flipping the top card to determine trump.
- After each trick, both players draw one card (winner draws first) until the draw pile is exhausted.
- During the draw phase, players need not follow suit or trump — any card may be played.
- Melding occurs after **each trick** (not in a separate phase) during the draw phase.
- Once the draw pile is exhausted, normal trick-taking rules (must follow suit, must trump) apply.
- There is no bidding; the player who wins the first trick leads.

---

## Variant: 3-Player Pinochle

- Deal 15 cards to each player, with **3 cards** forming a face-down **widow**.
- Each player bids; the highest bidder takes the widow, names trump, and discards 3 cards (discarded cards count as tricks won by the bidder for scoring purposes).
- No passing between players.
- All other rules apply as in the 4-player game.
